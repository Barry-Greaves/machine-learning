Mixing and Mastering

Learn how to mix like a pro

Mixing down a track is a dark art. Often, achieving the perfect mixdown is the culmination of hundreds of small decisions that combine to give you a professional sound, and when you nail these your can take your music to the next level.

It's the high-frequency sparkle on the track, it's that low-end thump that's hard to achieve - all of this comes down to the mix.

With 8 hours of tuition on topics ranging from advanced tips on working with EQs and compression, through to industry advice and knowledge on how to achieve the mixdown you're searching for, this course will improve your productions. Fact.

How mix, pan, gain-stage, compress and saturate your tracks to the next level

Overview

Mastering digital audio is a final check and coat of varnish on a mix before a song is called finished. A few of the main things mastering engineers do is make a track louder, both in volume as well as perceived volume, make a song sound better (which is, of course, subjective), and ultimately ensure a song translates to various playback systems.

Today, however, it's more and more common for mixers to do their own mastering in the box as well. This course will teach you all you need to know so you can master your own tracks and have them ready for streaming or playing live

Course Content

This wide ranging course provides essential training for the art of mixing and mastering.

Through effectively structured teaching on the fundamental tools and techniques, it aims to train aspiring mix engineers, music producers, composers and performers to achieve a professional finishing to their work on digital audio workstations (DAWs).

The knowledge and skills covered in this course are fully transferable (can be applied to any software/hardware).

How it works

The video lessons are available to stream from our website at any time and you can rewatch the content as many times as you need.

The course is split into three modules and their is a project submisssion at the end of each of them. One of our tutors will review your work and provide you with invaluable feedback on any areas you need to improve or focus on.
